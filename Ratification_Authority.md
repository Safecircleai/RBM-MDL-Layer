# Ratification Authority — Recognition Record

**Status:** RECOGNITION RECORD. This document records, for the purpose of this repository's constitutional
ratifications, **who currently occupies the Human Ratifying Authority (HRA) role** and how that occupancy
succeeds. It is **not** an ecosystem-governance definition: RBM-MDL-Layer *recognizes and defers to* this
authority; it does not own, grant, or define it (`MDL_Truth_Ownership_Matrix.md` — ecosystem/assistant
governance doctrine is not this node's to own). The authoritative definition and any succession live with the
ecosystem and the **Foundation Charter**, external to this repository.

**Why this record exists (and why it is not new doctrine):** every constitutional document in this repository
defers ratification to "the Human Ratifying Authority" — a *role* defined in `MDL_Consumer_Model.md` §1. Until
now the role had no recorded occupant. The occupant has been declared by the authority itself; recording it
here binds nothing new — it names the occupant of an already-defined role, which the independence model
explicitly accommodates ("roles bind; occupants illustrate; the domain stands alone").

---

## 1. The declaration (as received from the authority)

**Interim (present):** Until the Foundation and its permanent governance bodies are formally constituted, the
**Founder serves as the Human Ratifying Authority (HRA)**. The Founder possesses the **exclusive** authority
to:
- ratify constitutional decisions,
- resolve constitutional ambiguities, and
- authorize amendments to the Immutable Core.

**Permanent (upon establishment of the Foundation):** ratification authority transfers according to the
**Foundation Charter**. The Founder may retain a reserved constitutional role if desired; routine
constitutional governance becomes **institutional rather than personal**.

---

## 2. How this maps onto the existing role model

This declaration is an **occupant + succession** statement for a role the constitution already defines; it
changes no clause:

| Role (binding) | Occupant — interim | Occupant — permanent |
|---|---|---|
| **Human Ratifying Authority** (`MDL_Consumer_Model.md` §1: "the only role that may amend this constitution … a human role by definition") | **The Founder** (exclusive) | **The Foundation + its governing board**, per the Foundation Charter; the Founder may hold a reserved role |

The ten-year / occupant-replacement test (`Repository_Independence_Review.md` §4) still passes: substitute
Founder → Foundation and **not one constitutional clause requires rewriting**, because every clause binds the
*role*, never the occupant. This record is the illustration; the role is the doctrine.

## 4. What this record does not do

- It does **not** ratify anything — recording the authority is not exercising it.
- It does **not** define ecosystem or Foundation governance — that is external to this node; this record
  *defers* to the Foundation Charter and will be superseded by it on the permanent transfer.
- It does **not** hardwire "Founder" into any binding clause — the Founder is the *occupant* of the HRA role;
  the role remains the binding construct, and the occupant is replaceable by construction.
- It introduces **no new authority** for this node — MDL still owns only resolved-mandate truth
  (`Immutable_Core_Charter.md` §5); the HRA's authority is the HRA's, recognized here, not delegated to MDL.
